# Doctoral Program — 3-Year Schedule

**Professor Maine | Evangel University**  
**Fall 2026 – Summer 2029**

---

## Overview

This document provides a comprehensive semester-by-semester schedule for completing a doctoral program in three years. This framework reflects standard milestones across most doctoral programs: coursework, comprehensive examinations, dissertation proposal, research, and final defense.

> **Note:** Review and adapt this schedule with your faculty advisor in Fall 2026 to align with your specific program handbook.

---

## Year 1 — Foundations & Coursework (2026–2027)

Year 1 focuses on orientation to doctoral-level scholarship, beginning coursework, forming key faculty relationships, and laying the groundwork for a research area. Target: complete approximately one-third of required coursework and identify a preliminary research focus.

### Fall 2026 — Program Entry

| Area | Details |
|---|---|
| **Focus** | Program Entry & Orientation |
| **Credit Hours** | 6–9 (2–3 core courses) |

**Key Activities:**
- Attend doctoral orientation and program kickoff
- Meet with faculty advisor to review and sign degree plan
- Enroll in 2–3 core courses
- Review program handbook and dissertation requirements
- Join scholarly professional organizations in your field
- Set up Zotero for citation management
- Configure Instapaper and @Voice Aloud Reader (see [tools guide](../docs/tools-setup.md))

**Milestones / Deliverables:**
- [ ] Signed degree plan on file with advisor
- [ ] Established bi-weekly meeting cadence with advisor
- [ ] Annotated bibliography started (minimum 10 sources)

---

### Spring 2027 — Literature Immersion

| Area | Details |
|---|---|
| **Focus** | Coursework & Literature Immersion |
| **Credit Hours** | 6–9 (2–3 core courses) |

**Key Activities:**
- Continue core coursework
- Attend at least one academic conference or symposium
- Read seminal literature in your research area using Adler method (see [reading worksheet](../docs/adler-reading-worksheet.md))
- Identify potential dissertation committee members
- Draft a preliminary research interest statement (3–5 pages)
- Complete any required research methods or statistics coursework

**Milestones / Deliverables:**
- [ ] Research interest statement reviewed by advisor
- [ ] Provisional list of dissertation committee candidates
- [ ] Conference attendance or professional development log updated

---

### Summer 2027 — Independent Study & Research Prep

| Area | Details |
|---|---|
| **Focus** | Independent Study & Research Prep |
| **Credit Hours** | 3 (independent study or elective) |

**Key Activities:**
- Complete summer reading list: 15–20 foundational texts in your area
- Log all reading sessions in independent study weekly log
- Begin drafting a literature review outline
- Attend summer institute or workshop if available
- Review comprehensive exam requirements with advisor
- Draft Year 2 course plan and confirm committee members

**Milestones / Deliverables:**
- [ ] Literature review outline (10–15 pages)
- [ ] Finalized committee membership confirmed
- [ ] Year 2 plan approved by advisor

---

## Year 2 — Advanced Study & Qualifying Exams (2027–2028)

Year 2 marks the transition from student to scholar. The focus shifts from consuming knowledge to producing it. Key milestones: completing coursework, passing comprehensive exams, and defending the dissertation proposal.

### Fall 2027 — Advanced Coursework

| Area | Details |
|---|---|
| **Focus** | Advanced Coursework & Committee Work |
| **Credit Hours** | 6–9 |

**Key Activities:**
- Complete advanced coursework
- Formally convene dissertation committee (first official meeting)
- Expand literature review to 40–50 sources
- Identify research methodology and design framework
- Submit any advancement-to-candidacy forms
- Present research interest at departmental colloquium (if available)

**Milestones / Deliverables:**
- [ ] Official committee formation paperwork filed
- [ ] Expanded literature review draft (25–30 pages)
- [ ] Research methodology framework documented

---

### Spring 2028 — Comprehensive Exams & Proposal Writing

| Area | Details |
|---|---|
| **Focus** | Comprehensive Exams & Proposal Writing |
| **Credit Hours** | 6–9 (final semester of classes) |

**Key Activities:**
- Complete remaining required coursework
- Prepare for and sit comprehensive/qualifying examinations
- Begin writing Chapter 1 (Introduction) and Chapter 2 (Literature Review)
- Develop research questions and theoretical framework
- Schedule dissertation proposal defense (late spring or early summer)
- Apply for IRB approval if needed

**Milestones / Deliverables:**
- [ ] **Comprehensive examinations PASSED** ✓
- [ ] Dissertation proposal draft (Chapters 1–2 + methodology overview)
- [ ] IRB application submitted or approved

---

### Summer 2028 — Proposal Defense & ABD Status

| Area | Details |
|---|---|
| **Focus** | Proposal Defense & ABD Status |
| **Credit Hours** | Dissertation hours begin |

**Key Activities:**
- Defend dissertation proposal before committee
- Revise proposal based on committee feedback
- Achieve All But Dissertation (ABD) status
- Finalize IRB approval and research instruments
- Begin data collection or archival/primary source work

**Milestones / Deliverables:**
- [ ] **Proposal defense PASSED** ✓
- [ ] **ABD status officially conferred** ✓
- [ ] Data collection commenced
- [ ] IRB fully approved

---

## Year 3 — Dissertation Research, Writing & Defense (2028–2029)

Year 3 is defined by focused research, disciplined writing, and the final defense. Requires close committee communication and consistent daily writing habits.

### Fall 2028 — Data Collection & Analysis

| Area | Details |
|---|---|
| **Focus** | Data Collection & Analysis |
| **Credit Hours** | Dissertation hours |

**Key Activities:**
- Complete primary data collection
- Analyze data using identified research methodology
- Write Chapter 3 (Methodology) in full
- Begin writing Chapter 4 (Results/Findings)
- Attend dissertation writing group or boot camp
- Present at one national/regional academic conference
- Schedule mid-year committee check-in

**Milestones / Deliverables:**
- [ ] **Data collection COMPLETED** ✓
- [ ] Full Chapter 3 draft submitted to advisor
- [ ] Chapter 4 draft (partial) reviewed by committee
- [ ] Conference presentation delivered

---

### Spring 2029 — Dissertation Writing & Committee Review

| Area | Details |
|---|---|
| **Focus** | Dissertation Writing & Committee Review |
| **Credit Hours** | Dissertation hours |

**Key Activities:**
- Complete Chapters 4 and 5 (Findings & Discussion/Conclusions)
- Submit full dissertation draft to committee for review
- Incorporate committee feedback and revise all chapters
- Finalize front matter (abstract, acknowledgments, table of contents)
- Submit dissertation to graduate school for format review
- Schedule final dissertation defense (April or May 2029)

**Milestones / Deliverables:**
- [ ] Full dissertation draft submitted to committee
- [ ] Format check passed by graduate school
- [ ] Defense date scheduled and publicly announced

---

### Summer 2029 — Defense & Graduation

| Area | Details |
|---|---|
| **Focus** | Defense & Graduation |
| **Credit Hours** | Dissertation hours |

**Key Activities:**
- Complete final revisions based on committee direction
- Defend dissertation publicly before committee
- Submit final approved dissertation to graduate school repository
- Complete all graduation paperwork and clearances
- Apply for graduation (Summer or Fall 2029 commencement)
- Update CV and begin post-doctoral career steps

**Milestones / Deliverables:**
- [ ] **Dissertation defense PASSED** ✓
- [ ] Final dissertation submitted to repository
- [ ] **Degree conferred — Dr. Maine** 🎓
