Overview

This document provides a comprehensive semester-by-semester schedule for completing a doctoral program in three years, beginning Fall 2026. While specific program structures vary by institution and discipline (EdD, PhD), this framework reflects standard milestones shared across most doctoral programs: coursework, comprehensive examinations, dissertation proposal, research, and final defense.

The schedule is designed to be adapted to your specific program requirements. Review it with your faculty advisor during your first semester and adjust timelines to match your program's handbook.

Year 1 — Foundations & Coursework (2026–2027)

Year 1 focuses on orientation to doctoral-level scholarship, beginning coursework, forming key relationships with faculty, and laying the groundwork for your research area. By the end of Year 1, you should have completed approximately one-third of required coursework and identified a preliminary research focus.

Tips for Doctoral Success

Advising & Relationships

Your relationship with your faculty advisor is the single most important factor in doctoral completion. Meet regularly (at minimum bi-weekly), come prepared with written agendas, and keep a shared log of decisions and action items. Communicate proactively — silence is the most common source of delays.

Build strong relationships with your entire committee. Their collective expertise shapes your research and their signatures unlock every major milestone.

Writing Habits

Doctoral completion depends on writing consistency more than writing talent. Commit to daily writing — even 30 minutes at the same time each day — beginning in Year 1. Build the dissertation from your coursework papers, literature reviews, and seminar essays rather than treating it as a separate project that begins after classes end.

Use a citation manager from Day 1. Retrofitting citations is among the most frustrating dissertation tasks. Zotero (free, open source) integrates well with Word and Google Docs.

Managing the Timeline

Three-year completion is achievable but requires disciplined time management, especially given existing teaching responsibilities at Evangel. Key considerations:

  • Summer terms are often the most productive writing periods — protect them from overcommitment
  • Teaching loads and service commitments will compete with dissertation time in Years 2–3; negotiate course releases if possible
  • Build buffer into every milestone — plan for committee review to take 4 weeks even if you hope for 2
  • Set internal deadlines 2–3 weeks ahead of any official deadlines

Technology & Research Tools

Given your CS background and GitHub workflows, consider:

  • Using Git to version-control your dissertation chapters — especially any data analysis scripts or code-adjacent research
  • Leveraging your existing technical strengths to strengthen your research methodology (computational methods, data analysis)
  • Exploring open-access publishing and pre-print repositories (arXiv, ERIC, OSF) to build your scholarly presence during the program
  • Using your cybersecurity expertise as a lens for research in CS education — it's a distinguishing angle that connects teaching and scholarship
