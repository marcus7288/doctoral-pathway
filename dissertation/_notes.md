## Research Focus

**Primary question (developing):** How can Missiology frameworks of contextualization inform CS pedagogy in faith-based higher education contexts?

This question draws on:
- M.A. Missiology / Intercultural Communications (AGTS) — contextualization theory, intercultural communication frameworks
- CS education research (SIGCSE, ICER) — pedagogy, curriculum design, equity
- Faith-based institutional context — Assemblies of God, Christian liberal arts, theological integration

## Candidate Dissertation Topics

| Topic | Description | Key Strength |
|---|---|---|
| **A — Faith-integrated CS pedagogy** | How Missiology frameworks of contextualization can inform CS pedagogy in faith-based HE | Direct intersection of both graduate credentials |
| **B — Cybersecurity curriculum for AG-affiliated institutions** | Curricular design for cybersecurity at Assemblies of God-affiliated colleges | Addresses real gap; empirical curriculum research possible |
| **C — CS equity in Christian liberal arts contexts** | Access, retention, and equity in CS at faith-based institutions | Growing national concern; Margolis/Fisher frameworks apply |
| **D — AI literacy frameworks for Christian higher education** | What AI literacy means in a theological anthropology framework | Timely; connects to 6.7960 deep learning OCW course |

**Decision target:** Narrow to 1–2 candidates by June 2027, confirm single topic by Fall 2027 with advisor.

### Notes on each topic

**Topic A** is the most natural synthesis of the two graduate credentials. The risk is that it may be too broad — needs to be scoped to a specific pedagogical intervention or institutional type.

**Topic B** has the most direct connection to current teaching practice and has a clear empirical research design available (curriculum audit + faculty/student survey at AG institutions). This is the most actionable dissertation.

**Topic C** connects strongly to the Margolis and Fisher (Unlocking the Clubhouse) literature already in the reading list. Risk: equity research in CS is very competitive field; must have a distinctive faith-context angle.

**Topic D** is the most timely, but the field is moving fast and a dissertation begun in 2029 risks finding that the landscape has shifted. The theological anthropology angle (what does it mean to be human in relation to AI, from a Christian perspective) is distinctive.

## Preliminary Research Questions

> *To be refined in consultation with faculty advisor. These are working questions only.*

**If Topic A:**
- How do Missiology frameworks of contextualization translate into concrete pedagogical strategies for CS instruction in faith-based contexts?
- What does culturally and theologically situated CS education look like in practice at AG-affiliated institutions?

**If Topic B:**
- What cybersecurity curriculum models exist at AG-affiliated institutions, and how do they compare to secular equivalents?
- How do faculty at faith-based institutions integrate vocational calling and cybersecurity stewardship into course design?

**If Topic C:**
- What structural and cultural barriers to CS access exist specifically in Christian liberal arts contexts?
- How do equity frameworks developed in secular institutions (Margolis, Fisher) apply or fail to apply in faith-based contexts?

**If Topic D:**
- What theological and epistemological frameworks should ground AI literacy education in Christian higher education?
- How do Christian colleges currently approach AI in curriculum, and what are the gaps?
