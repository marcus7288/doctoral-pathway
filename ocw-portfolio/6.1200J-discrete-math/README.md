## Why this course

Discrete mathematics is the foundational gap to close before any doctoral-level CS coursework. Every algorithm, proof, and formal argument in the courses that follow builds on this. The number theory and cryptography content connects directly to my cybersecurity teaching at Evangel, moving it from practitioner-level to academically rigorous.

---

## Topics covered

- Propositions and logical notation
- Sets, functions, and relations
- Mathematical induction and proofs
- Graph theory and state machines
- Recurrences and asymptotic notation
- Number theory and cryptography
- Permutations, combinations, and discrete probability

## Connection to research

The modular arithmetic and number theory content directly supports teaching *why* RSA encryption works — moving the cybersecurity course from practitioner-level to academically grounded. Proof-writing methods also provide the language needed to engage with CS education research literature at a graduate level, particularly in formal methods and algorithm analysis papers.

The logic and formal notation connects directly to Dissertation Topic B (cybersecurity curriculum for AG-affiliated institutions) — a rigorous mathematical foundation is the argument for why a faith-based institution should teach theory, not just tools.
